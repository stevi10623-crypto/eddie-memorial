Edward "Fast Eddie" Cota

1965 — 2025

The Man • The Myth • The Legend

Scroll for the Legend

The Soul

Edward Andre Cota was more than a father, a husband, or a friend—he was a force of nature. Born in 1965 and raised in the heart of the San Gabriel Valley, Eddie grew up a proud son of El Monte, California. He graduated from El Monte High School with the Class of '83, then went on to study at Rio Hondo College. But his real education came from the streets, the stages, and the people of Los Angeles.

Known to the world as 'Fast Eddie,' he earned the nickname not just for his quick wit, but for the speed at which he could close a deal, make a friend, or turn a total stranger into family. He spent his career dominating the HVAC/R industry—first at United Refrigeration and later at Allied Refrigeration, where he was consistently one of the top sales professionals in the region. His clients weren't just customers—they were friends for life.

Eddie was unapologetically himself: hilarious, loud, impossibly stylish, and fiercely loyal to his people. He was a lifelong LA Dodgers fanatic who bled blue, a die-hard rock and metal head who worshipped Ozzy, Dio, and Tool. Whether he was front row at the House of Blues or holding court at a backyard carne asada, Eddie was always the brightest light in the room.

The Family Man

At the center of Eddie's world was his family. His wife Lydia was his rock, his partner, and his everything. Together they built a life overflowing with love, laughter, and loyalty. Eddie was the devoted father of two beautiful daughters: Erica and Antoinette—each one carrying a piece of his fire, his humor, and his heart.

As a grandfather, Eddie was pure magic. He was the loudest voice in the room at every birthday, the first to volunteer for babysitting duty, and the one who could make any kid laugh no matter what. His grandson Mateo held a special place in his heart—Eddie supported him through every challenge with relentless love and an unshakable spirit that inspired everyone around him.

Devoted Husband

Eddie and Lydia were inseparable—partners in crime, best friends, and soulmates. He never missed a chance to make her laugh or remind her she was the most important person in his world.

Proud Father

Erica and Antoinette were his greatest achievements. He raised his two daughters with tough love, endless humor, and the kind of loyalty that only a Cota could deliver.

The Best Grandpa

Whether it was making silly faces, sneaking extra treats, or just being present—Eddie made every grandchild feel like the most important person on the planet. Mateo's biggest champion, without question.

Rock & Roll

House of Blues

Eddie was a regular at the House of Blues on the Sunset Strip. He lived for the live shows—front row, cold beer in hand, losing himself in the music. From Ozzy to Dio, that stage was his church and every concert was a sermon.

Sunset Strip Legend

The Comedy Store

Eddie wasn't just a regular—he was family at the legendary Comedy Store on the Sunset Strip. He knew the doormen, the comics, the bartenders. When he passed, the Store put his name on the marquee—a final standing ovation for the man who always had the best seat in the house and the loudest laugh in the room.

Bleed Blue

Die-Hard Dodger Fan

Eddie bled Dodger blue from birth. He never missed a game on TV and lit up every time he walked into Dodger Stadium. Win or lose, he repped LA with pride—foam finger and all. The Dodgers weren't just a team to Eddie, they were a way of life.
